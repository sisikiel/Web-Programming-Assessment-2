Asthma

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

COPD includes conditions like chronic bronchitis and emphysema. It leads to difficulty breathing, persistent cough, and frequent respiratory infections.

Pneumonia

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs and may cause them to fill with fluid. Symptoms include sharp chest pain, high fever, and deep cough.

Lung Cancer

Lung cancer can develop in any part of the lungs and is often linked to smoking. Symptoms are a chronic cough, coughing up blood, and unexplained weight loss.

Pulmonary Fibrosis

This condition involves progressive scarring of lung tissue, leading to increasing difficulty in breathing. Causes may include environmental factors and autoimmune diseases.
